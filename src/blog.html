Game design for VR games: features and recommendations

Game Design June 08 2022

My name is Anton Saturday, I have been working as a game designer for 7 years. In this article I will talk about the features of VR games using the example of our large VR project Into the Radius. I've been working on it as the main game designer for the last 2 years, so there will be a lot of details!

The article will be useful for those who want to better understand the development of VR games, compare them with regular games, find out non-obvious features and how to adapt them for VR.

Unlike "flat" games (on a monitor or TV), VR games work differently. The player has a helmet through which he looks into the game world, and controllers that follow the movements of his physical hands. In such games, everything that happens is seen from the player's point of view and is perceived more personal. But this creates new and unusual problems for the developers of such games.

Differences between VR games and regular games

Because of this, it is extremely difficult to make cut-scenes, since the classic techniques are not applicable. For example, a span of cameras, a change of angle, color filters. The player can be rocked.

Virtual hands mimic the movements of physical hands.

Due to their design, VR controllers convey interaction with the game's environment more believably than a keyboard or gamepad.

For example, VR controllers have a dedicated grab button. Pressing is pretty close to real grasping. Let the simplification, but sufficient to create the right sensations.

Differences between VR games and regular games

Obviously, these are not all features, since I only worked on shooter, then new problems will come out in other genres. Besides, something I definitely missed it. But I hope now you understand how much VR games are different, and that they require different approaches to development games. Of course, something can be taken from "flat" games, but in VR it is very many unique moments.

Many things have to be reinvented - interfaces, interaction With environment, movement mechanics. You can't blindly take established elements from regular games and add to the VR game. A lot will have to prototype and experiment to build mechanics into virtual space is harmonious. Because often the mechanics on paper will feel different in VR space.

Another important thing is that VR games are much closer to the player, any fears and physiological characteristics come to the fore. Therefore, it is important test the game on different people so that as soon as possible discover problem areas.
